# Chapter 011 — The Betrayer's Blade
## Act 03: Betrayal

### Scene: Crimson Fang Outpost — Twilight

Beyond the capital gates, the road splits at a stone watchtower. Jin follows torchlight and the scent of iron — and walks into an ambush laid by assassins who knew he was coming.

A wraith materializes behind the last bandit, untouched by arrow or blade. The Fang is raising the dead — but not like the skeletons at the gate. *These* were consumed whole.

### Dialogue

**JIN**
> Past the gates. The Crimson Fang's territory... and they're ready for me.

**ASSASSIN**
> The Sword of Ruin. The Master warned us you were coming.

**JIN**
> Wraiths. They weren't at the gate — someone summoned them here.

**JIN**
> That wraith's blade was forged from the consumed. Spirit Edge may be the only thing that cuts clean through.

### Gameplay

- **Objective**: Survive 3 waves, ending in a wraith-only assault
- **Difficulty**: Mid-Act 3 — bandit/assassin warmup, then wraith pressure
- **Wraith stats**: HP 70-75, spd 65-70, dmg 22-24 (life-drain threat)
- **Reward**: Spirit Edge (ghost-forged blade) — first weapon with both Uncommon damage AND fast cooldown
- **Variance**: Assassins will vanish mid-wave; player needs the dodge window

### Design Notes
- Wraiths are the new enemy faction (per Act 3 brief: "story twist, new enemy faction")
- 3 bandits → 1 wraith + bandits → 3 wraiths (escalation)
- No merchant ally — Jin is alone inside enemy territory
- Wave 2's bandit is intentionally tougher (HP 75) to test Spirit Edge pickup timing
- The wraith-only wave 3 is a stat-check before the boss

### Layout
```
Screen 640x360

Wave 1:  Bandits flank (150,110) (520,130) — Assassin middle (330,80)
Wave 2:  Wraiths spread (220,200) (460,220) — Bandit back (380,100)
Wave 3:  Wraith triangle (180,120) (480,140) (320,250)
```

### Narrative Beat
The merchant's wagon fortress held. The capital gates are open. But Act 3 is the moment Jin learns the Crimson Fang isn't a gang — it's a *cult*, and they've learned to bind wraiths to their service. The betrayal isn't that someone sold him out. The betrayal is that the dead themselves now serve the Fang.
