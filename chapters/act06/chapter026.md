# Chapter 026 — What Comes After
## Act 06: Prophecy

### Scene: The Tower Ruins — Dawn

The master is dead. The tower is silent. The alliance waits outside.

Jin sits in the master's chair, the Sword of Ruin in his hand — but it is no longer cold. The blade glows faintly red. The visions begin.

The priest's voice, the first time since her death. She explains: the prophecy was never about the Crimson Fang. The Fang was a symptom. The prophecy is about the sword itself — *Remembrance*, an ancient blade forged before the empire, which carries the memory of every wielder it has ever had.

The sword is showing Jin its memories. The Fang's dead are still here, in the blade's memory.

### Dialogue

**JIN**
> The tower is silent. The master is dead. But the sword in my hand burns — it's showing me something. Visions of what comes next.

**PRIEST**
> Jin. The prophecy was never about the Fang. It was about the sword. The sword that was forged before the empire. The sword that remembers.

**JIN**
> The sword has a name. I hear it now. *Remembrance*. It wants me to see what it has seen.

**MERCHANT**
> The sword speaks to you. I always knew it would. The priest told me, before the Fang killed her.

### Gameplay
- **Objective**: Survive the sword's first vision-trial in 3 waves
- **Difficulty**: Act 6 opens with the merchant as the only ally — Jin is alone in the vision
- **Reward**: 800 XP, 350g

### Design Notes
- The first chapter where the player *knows* the sword has a name
- The priest's voice is the first time a dead character speaks — the sword's memory preserves her
- The void theme (deep purple/black) is the visual identity for all of Act 6
