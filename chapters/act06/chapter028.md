# Chapter 028 — The Second Memory
## Act 06: Prophecy

### Scene: The Second Memory — The Broken Empire

The first wielder is dead. His empire fell. The sword passed to a betrayer — a man who broke the world the king had forged, because he believed the world was a lie.

The betrayer is the second memory. He is the wraith that the sword carries: a man of conviction so twisted that he ended an age to prove a point. The sword remembers him too.

### Dialogue

**JIN**
> The second memory. The first wielder is dead — his empire broke. The sword passed to a betrayer. He used it to shatter the world the king had forged.

**PRIEST**
> The sword remembers the betrayer. It was made to remember. That is the prophecy — that the sword would find a wielder who would end what the betrayer started.

**BETRAYER**
> I broke the world because the world was a lie. The sword was the only truth. I wonder, Jin — do you have the strength to break it again?

**JIN**
> The second memory fades. The betrayer is dead in the sword's memory. The world is not a lie — I will not break it. I will keep it.

### Gameplay
- **Objective**: Survive the second memory-trial in 3 waves
- **Difficulty**: Act 6 mid-act (3 assassins in wave 1, betrayer + golem in wave 3)
- **Reward**: 1000 XP, 420g
- **Boss wave**: The betrayer (wraith with HP 360) + golem (HP 320)

### Design Notes
- The betrayer's "do you have the strength to break it again" is the central question of Act 6
- The priest finally explains the prophecy directly: the sword was made to find a wielder who would *end* what the betrayer started
- Jin's "I will keep it" line is the first explicit rejection of the prophecy's destructive purpose
