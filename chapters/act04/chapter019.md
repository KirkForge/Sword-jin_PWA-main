# Chapter 019 — The Alliance Marches
## Act 04: Alliance

### Scene: The Southern Provinces — March

The alliance crosses the provincial border at dawn. Three banners catch the wind. The Fang's outer provinces, long-held by bandit garrisons and Fang preachers, fall one by one through the day.

By dusk, the alliance has fought three engagements: a provincial garrison, a Fang counter-attack, and a wraith vanguard. The southern capital rises on the horizon.

### Dialogue

**JIN**
> Three banners. The merchant's caravan, the imperial garrison, Kaelen's riders. The Fang's outer provinces fall in a day.

**KAELEN**
> The necromancer is mine. Let the Sword of Ruin handle the assassins — the old bandit lord has scores to settle.

**JIN**
> Wraith vanguard. The Fang's pulling out their best for this. Good — let them.

**MERCHANT**
> The southern provinces are ours. Tomorrow we see the Fang's capital with our own eyes.

### Gameplay
- **Objective**: Lead the alliance through 3 battles in one day
- **Difficulty**: Act 4 peak — full alliance deployed
- **Wave 2 (Fang counter)**: First chapter with a *caster enemy* (necromancer) AND assassins together
- **Wave 3 (wraith vanguard)**: 3 wraiths at HP 120 — the "Act 4 horde"
- **Reward**: 500 XP, 200g

### Design Notes
- The first chapter where Jin's party is at *full strength* (merchant + 2 soldiers + bandit king)
- Kaelen's "the necromancer is mine" line is a hint that party members can target priority enemies (combat AI is a future system, not implemented yet — the hint is for Act 5/6)
- The "wraith vanguard" wave 3 is a soft tutorial for Act 5's army-vs-army sections

### Narrative Beat
The alliance's first real march. Three battles, three banners, one enemy. The southern capital is in sight. The Fang's master is in that capital. Act 4 is the moment the player stops being "Jin, the swordsman" and becomes "Commander Jin, banner of the alliance."
