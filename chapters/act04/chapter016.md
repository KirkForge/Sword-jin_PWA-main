# Chapter 016 — The Imperial Seal
## Act 04: Alliance

### Scene: The Gate Warden's Hold — Dawn

Jin rides south with the merchant. At the imperial fortress, the gate warden who was wounded at the capital gates (ch010) is now standing, recovered, holding court. The warden's golem stands at the fortress entrance — a relic of the old empire, still functional.

The imperial court has noticed Jin's killing of the Fang priest. They want to talk.

### Dialogue

**WARDEN**
> The Sword of Ruin. The priest's death echoes through the imperial court. The throne takes notice.

**JIN**
> I didn't come for the throne's notice. I came for answers about the prophecy.

**WARDEN**
> Defeat my golem without breaking. Then the seal is yours — and an army answers when you call.

**JIN**
> The warden's seal. Now the southern roads answer to me — and the imperial army stands with us.

### Gameplay
- **Objective**: Defeat the warden's golem in a 3-wave fight (Fang vanguard → wraiths → golem)
- **Difficulty**: Mid-Act 4, party formation
- **Golem stats**: HP 220, spd 50, dmg 36, armor 12 (highest HP enemy in the game)
- **Reward**: 350 XP, 120g, **Imperial Seal** (passive — allies gain +20% damage when player crits)

### Design Notes
- The merchant (ch015) returns as the player's first permanent ally
- 3-wave structure tests the full Act 3 toolkit: bandits, wraiths, then a stat-check on the boss
- The "defeat without breaking" framing means the golem fight is intentionally hard but fair — Spirit Edge + Shadow Step counters golem armor

### Narrative Beat
Act 4 opens with the imperial court recognizing Jin. The warden's seal is the player's first real political power — not just a sword, but a *banner*. The southern roads now answer to Jin, not just to the merchant and the player.
