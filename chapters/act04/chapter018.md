# Chapter 018 — The Bandit King's Surrender
## Act 04: Alliance

### Scene: The Old Fort — Dusk

The bandit king Kaelen Storm once led every outlaw between the eastern gate and the river. The Fang bought his sword for gold; now the imperial court has bought it back with proof of strength.

Kaelen asks for trial by combat. His warband against Jin's alliance. The last one standing earns the right to recruit the other.

### Dialogue

**BANDIT_KING**
> I am Kaelen Storm. I led the eastern bandits until the Fang bought my sword. Now I lead them for you — if you'll have me.

**JIN**
> Prove it. Your warband against my blade. The last one standing earns the right to ask.

**BANDIT_KING**
> Then we fight as brothers. Come, Sword of Ruin — let the eastern roads see who truly rules them.

**JIN**
> Kaelen kneels. The eastern bandit clans are ours now. Three banners, three armies, one march south.

### Gameplay
- **Objective**: Defeat the bandit king's warband (3 waves: bandits → assassins → berserker king)
- **Difficulty**: Stacked Act 4 stats
- **Berserker king (final)**: HP 180, spd 95, dmg 40 — most dangerous single enemy so far
- **Reward**: 460 XP, 180g, **Kaelen Storm joins the party** (ally with combat AI, can be assigned to skill slot 3)

### Design Notes
- The bandit king's berserker is a stat-check designed to test Spirit Edge + Rally Cry timing
- Killing him is optional — the dialogue implies Jin can "surrender" instead (a future dialogue branch could lead to a different act 4 ending)
- Kaelen joining as a 3rd party member is the alliance's full strength: merchant (healer) + imperial soldiers (tank) + bandit king (damage)

### Narrative Beat
Kaelen's recruitment is the alliance's *political* arrival. The Fang bought him once with gold; Jin buys him with honor. The southern march now has three banners under one sword.
